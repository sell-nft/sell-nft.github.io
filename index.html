01 — Overview

What does it mean to sell an NFT?

To sell an NFT means listing a unique blockchain token for purchase, then transferring it from your wallet to the buyer when the sale settles.

When you sell NFT inventory, the asset usually stays in your self-custody wallet until a marketplace sale, accepted offer, or auction settlement moves it to a buyer. The listing defines the sale format, accepted currency, price, duration, and any marketplace terms. Wallets approve the marketplace contract to handle the specific NFT or collection, while the blockchain records ownership transfer after settlement. Vendora treats the process as a seller workflow, not an investment promise.

02 — How it works

How to sell an NFT

The usual way to sell an NFT is to connect a wallet, list the item, approve the marketplace contract, and complete the sale or accept an offer.

Start by confirming the NFT, collection contract, chain, and wallet address. Then choose a marketplace that supports that chain and listing type. Fixed-price listings are straightforward; auctions and offers can work better when buyer demand is uncertain. Before signing, review token approvals, gas prompts, seller fees, creator royalties, payout currency, and expiration. A legitimate marketplace should never ask for your seed phrase.

  1. 1
    Connect your wallet

    Open the marketplace from a verified URL, connect the wallet that holds the NFT, and confirm the site is reading the correct chain and collection.

  2. 2
    List the NFT

    Choose a fixed price, auction, or offer-based sale. Set duration, payout currency, and any listing terms the marketplace requires.

  3. 3
    Set price and approve

    Compare active listings and recent demand without assuming a sale. Review token approvals, royalty handling, marketplace fees, and any gas prompt before signing.

  4. 4
    Accept a sale or offer

    When a buyer purchases or you accept an offer, confirm the wallet prompt, settlement currency, and final proceeds before the NFT transfers.

03 — Marketplaces

Where to sell an NFT

You can sell NFTs on general marketplaces, pro trading venues, and multichain marketplaces, depending on where buyers for that collection already shop.

OpenSea, Blur, Magic Eden, and Rarible are real NFT marketplaces sellers often compare. The best venue depends on collection demand, supported chain, listing tools, buyer behavior, fees, royalty handling, and wallet compatibility. Vendora can present marketplace choices as a selling checklist: where the NFT is recognized, where comparable listings exist, and where offers are active.

OpenSea

A broad marketplace for NFTs and tokenized assets, useful when a seller wants general discovery, collection pages, wallet-based listings, and familiar buyer behavior. Check supported chains, listing type, fees, and royalty handling before signing.

Blur

A marketplace built for pro-style NFT trading, with a stronger fit for collections that already have active bids, offers, and trader attention. Review offer terms carefully because fast execution does not remove approval or market risk.

Magic Eden

A multichain NFT marketplace with strong roots in collectible and gaming communities. It can be useful when the NFT's audience is concentrated on a specific chain, but sellers should confirm chain support and payout details.

Rarible

A multichain marketplace with creator-oriented listing flows and collection support. It can fit art, one-of-one work, and creator-led collections, provided the seller verifies the network, contract, fees, and wallet prompts.

04 — Compare

Choosing where to sell NFTs

Choosing where to sell is less about one universal winner and more about matching your NFT to the marketplace where buyers, chain support, and sale mechanics line up.

Compare marketplace type before you list. Generalist venues can be easier for broad discovery. Pro trading markets may suit liquid collections with active bids. Multichain marketplaces can help when the NFT is not on Ethereum mainnet or when the collection has a strong home-chain audience. Always re-check current fee, royalty, and chain support on the marketplace before signing a listing.

05 — Security

Safety, approvals & scams when you sell NFT

Selling safely means verifying the marketplace, understanding wallet signatures, limiting token approvals, and ignoring fake offers or surprise airdrops.

Use the marketplace URL directly or from a trusted bookmark, not a random ad, DM, or email link. Read wallet prompts before signing; a listing signature is different from a broad token approval. If you grant approvals, review and revoke old permissions when they are no longer needed. Treat fake collection offers, malicious airdrops, urgent buyer messages, and support impersonators as hostile until verified. Never share your seed phrase.

06 — Fees

Fees, royalties & gas when you sell an NFT

The cost to sell NFTs can include marketplace fees, creator royalties, and network gas, and each component can vary by marketplace, collection, chain, and market conditions.

Marketplace fees are charged by the venue or protocol. Creator royalties may be defined by the collection and handled differently across marketplaces. Network gas is paid to the blockchain validators or sequencers for on-chain actions such as approvals, transfers, or settlement. These costs can change, so the seller should review the final wallet prompt and marketplace confirmation screen before listing or accepting an offer.

07 — FAQ

FAQ: sell NFT basics

How do I sell an NFT?

To sell an NFT, use the wallet that holds it, open a verified marketplace, and choose a listing format such as fixed price, auction, or accepting offers. Review the collection, chain, price, currency, duration, marketplace fee, royalties, and wallet prompt before signing. If the item sells, the marketplace or protocol settles the trade and transfers the NFT to the buyer. Never enter your seed phrase to list or sell.

Where is the best place to sell NFTs?

There is no single best place to sell NFTs. The right marketplace depends on the NFT's chain, collection activity, buyer audience, listing tools, fees, royalties, and whether active offers exist. Generalist marketplaces can help with discovery, while trader-oriented venues may suit liquid collections. Multichain marketplaces can work better when the collection's buyers are concentrated outside Ethereum mainnet. Check where comparable NFTs are actually listed and receiving bids.

How much does it cost to sell an NFT?

The cost can include a marketplace fee, creator royalties, and network gas. The exact amount varies by marketplace, collection rules, chain, and current network conditions, so it should be checked at the moment you list or accept an offer. Some actions may require a wallet approval, and some sales settle with costs deducted from proceeds. Do not rely on old screenshots or third-party summaries for current fee details.

Do I pay gas to sell an NFT?

You may pay gas for certain wallet actions, approvals, transfers, or settlement steps, depending on the chain and marketplace design. Some listing signatures can be off-chain, while accepting an offer or approving a marketplace contract may require an on-chain transaction. Gas is not a marketplace promise; it is a network cost that can change. Always read the wallet prompt and confirm the chain before signing.

Can I sell an NFT with no buyers?

You can list an NFT even when there are no active buyers, but listing does not create demand. If there are no bids, you can use a fixed-price listing, start an auction, lower the price, promote the collection, or wait for offers. For illiquid NFTs, the best visible floor price may not be a realistic sale price. Treat offers, collection activity, and recent comparable sales as signals, not guarantees.

Is selling NFTs safe?

Selling NFTs can be done safely, but it requires wallet discipline. Use verified marketplace URLs, inspect every signature request, avoid links from unsolicited DMs, and revoke old token approvals when they are no longer needed. Do not trust surprise airdrops, fake buyer support chats, or urgent offers that push you to sign unfamiliar transactions. No legitimate marketplace needs your seed phrase to sell an NFT.
